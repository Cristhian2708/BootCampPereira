Wind power or wind energy is a form of renewable energy that harnesses the power of the wind to generate electricity. It involves using wind turbines to convert the turning motion of blades, pushed by moving air (kinetic energy) into electrical energy (electricity).

How does it work?

Wind turbines work on a simple principle: instead of using electricity to make windâ€”like a fanâ€”wind turbines use wind to make electricity. Wind turns the propeller-like blades of a turbine around a rotor, which spins a generator, which creates electricity.

Advantages

  • Wind is a clean source of renewable energy that produces no air or water pollution.
  • Wind is a domestic source of energy.
  • Wind is a free fuel source.
  • Wind turbines can be built on existing farms or ranches.
  • Wind power is cost-effective.

Current Implementation Of Wind Energy

Wind power is the fastest-growing source of electricity generation in the world, with the global installed wind power capacity increasing by 57.2 GW in 2017, and wind power generation increased by 17% compared to 2016. The global installed wind power capacity has increased by 10% in 2017, with 52.6 GW installed in 2017, bringing the total installed wind capacity to 539 GW.
